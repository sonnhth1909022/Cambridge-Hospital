ICU Room

Used for emergency situations.

The intensive care unit (ICU) room is a highly specialized environment, differing in many ways from a standard hospital room. ICU rooms are staffed with a higher nursing staffing ratio, typically one nurse to two rooms, and a premium is placed on patient visibility.

Units are often constructed in such a manner that all patients can be under continuous observation from the central-nursing station, either directly or using cameras. Patients are individually monitored with a variety of bedside physiologic monitors, and ICU rooms are designed to have redundant gas and electric sources.
